Portland International Tileset

James Clunes

This is a love letter to a five-year-old me, excited to sit next to the window, not in his mother's lap for the first time. Little did he know, when you make your own maps, every seat is the window seat.

The map takes cues from the frontage, terminals, and design aesthetic of Portland International. Dark blues and greens accented by light wood create a tie to the Pacific Northwest's natural beauty, while aspects of the built environment are represented the grays and yellows of the tarmac and runways.

Although he was carried through most of the security lines, he remembers the green carpet with blue and and maroon hatching.

At his gate, he looked across the gray tarmac crisscrossed by yellow centerlines and bright yellow taxiway signs. As a jet started its takeoff roll, his father made engine sounds.

After takeoff, the plane turned gently, and his father pointed out each mountain that passed by his window.

Places once far away became white names on a blue arrival board, soon to be visited!

Soon they were high above the earth, and silky clouds sat snuggled up in the sleepy valleys below.

Roads stretched into the coming dawn like necklaces of gold on a jade velvet bed.

As they descended into Phoenix, the city became a grid of concrete squares.

He saw the plane's shadow flitting over rooftops and parking lots below.

Since then, that boy has flown to many airports!

He's visited relatives in Boise...

Seen his Aunt and Uncle in Palm Springs...

Event went on a SCUBA diving trip in Cozumel...

But his favorite airport is the little Corvallis airport, where that boy has logged over 300 landings.
